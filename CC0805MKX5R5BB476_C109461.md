## Pin Descriptions

> MLCCs are **2-terminal, non-polarized** devices. There are no numbered pins — connections are made via the two end terminations.

| Terminal | Description |
|---|---|
| **Terminal 1** (Left end cap) | Electrode connection — either polarity |
| **Terminal 2** (Right end cap) | Electrode connection — either polarity |

> ⚠️ **No polarity.** X5R MLCCs are non-polarized; either terminal may connect to the positive or negative rail.

## Important Design Notes & Limitations

### ⚠️ DC Bias Derating (Critical)
- X5R dielectric exhibits **significant capacitance loss under DC bias**. At rated voltage (6.3V), effective capacitance can drop **30–70%** below nominal 47µF. Always verify effective capacitance at operating voltage using manufacturer's DC bias curves or simulation tools (e.g., YAGEO's online simulator).

### ⚠️ Voltage Margin
- Maximum rated voltage is **6.3V**. Do not exceed this value including transients. Voltage proof test is performed at **2.5× Ur = 15.75V** for 1–5 seconds.

### ⚠️ Temperature Derating
- Capacitance varies up to **±15%** across -55°C to +85°C operating range (characteristic bell curve peaking near +15°C).
- Do not use above **+85°C**.

### Soldering
- **Reflow soldering only** for 47µF / 0805 (>2.2µF in 0805 requires reflow; wave soldering not permitted).
- Preheat: 120°C–150°C for 1 minute; peak reflow: 260°C max.

### Mechanical
- Bending strength test: 1mm deflection; post-bend ΔC/C must remain within **±12.5%** (High Capacitance classification).
- Avoid mechanical stress after soldering; MLCC ceramic is brittle and susceptible to board flex cracking.

### Aging
- Class 2 (X5R) dielectrics exhibit **capacitance aging** over time (typically ~2–3% per decade hour loss). Capacitance recovers partially after heating above Curie temperature.

### Packing
- Blister tape on 7-inch reel: **3,000 pcs/reel** (BB = blister, R = 7-inch reel per ordering code).
