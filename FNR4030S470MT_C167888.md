## Typical Application Circuit

The FNR4030S470MT is used as a filter/energy storage inductor in DC-DC switching converter circuits.

```
        L1 (47µH)
Vin ──┬──[SW]────[FNR4030S470MT]────┬── Vout

      └──[D1]──────────────────────┤          ├──
```

**Typical Component Values for ~1 MHz Buck Converter:**

| Component | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| L1 | 47 µH | This inductor |

> **Note:** At 47 µH, this inductor is better suited for lower switching frequencies (100–500 kHz) due to its 8.4 MHz SRF.

## Important Design Notes & Limitations

1. **Isat Definition:** Isat is the DC current causing **~30% inductance drop** from zero-current value. Do not exceed 0.95 A (typ) to maintain inductive behavior.

2. **Irms Definition:** Irms is the DC current causing a **ΔT = +40°C** rise from 20°C ambient. The rated Irms of 0.80 A (typ) already accounts for self-heating.

3. **Rated Current = 0.80 A** — governed by the lesser of Isat and Irms; in this case Irms is the limiting factor.

4. **SRF Constraint:** The 8.4 MHz minimum SRF means the inductor becomes capacitive above this frequency. Switching frequency must remain well below SRF (recommend < SRF/10 for best performance).

5. **Inductance Tolerance:** ±20% — factor this into worst-case ripple current calculations.

6. **Temperature Derating:** Specifications are referenced to 20°C ambient. At elevated ambient temperatures (up to 125°C total including self-heating), derate current accordingly.

7. **Closed Magnetic Circuit:** The shielded construction minimizes EMI/leakage flux — suitable for noise-sensitive applications.

8. **Soldering:** SMD reflow compatible. Follow standard SMD soldering profiles; avoid mechanical stress on terminations after soldering.
